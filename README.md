## 📌 Project Overview

Customer retention is a critical challenge in the banking industry. Traditional financial indicators such as account balance and salary often fail to capture behavioral patterns that lead to customer churn.

This project focuses on analyzing **customer engagement** and **product utilization** to identify retention drivers and uncover high-risk customer segments. The goal is to develop a **data-driven retention strategy** using behavioral insights rather than relying solely on financial metrics.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

### Primary Objectives
- Evaluate the relationship between **customer engagement and churn**
- Analyze the impact of **product utilization on retention**
- Identify **high-value but disengaged customers**

### Secondary Objectives
- Support **engagement-driven retention strategies**
- Improve **cross-selling and product bundling decisions**
- Reduce **silent churn among premium customers**

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset includes customer-level banking information with the following key features:

- CustomerId – Unique identifier  
- CreditScore – Creditworthiness  
- Geography – Country (France, Spain, Germany)  
- Gender – Male/Female  
- Age – Customer age  
- Tenure – Years with bank  
- Balance – Account balance  
- NumOfProducts – Number of products used  
- HasCrCard – Credit card ownership  
- IsActiveMember – Activity status  
- EstimatedSalary – Annual salary  
- Exited – Churn indicator  

---

## 🧠 Analytical Methodology

The project follows a structured analytical framework:

### 1. Data Ingestion & Validation
- Data cleaning and validation  
- Ensuring consistency in binary variables  
- Verifying churn labels  

### 2. Engagement Classification
Customers are segmented into:
- Active engaged customers  
- Inactive disengaged customers  
- Active low-product customers  
- Inactive high-balance customers  

### 3. Product Utilization Analysis
- Churn rate by number of products  
- Single vs multi-product retention comparison  
- Product depth vs churn relationship  

### 4. Financial vs Engagement Analysis
- Balance vs activity comparison  
- Salary–balance mismatch detection  
- Identification of at-risk premium customers  

### 5. Retention Strength Assessment
- Sticky customer identification  
- Engagement threshold analysis  
- Relationship Strength Index development  

---

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- **Engagement Retention Ratio** → Active vs inactive churn comparison  
- **Product Depth Index** → Product usage vs loyalty  
- **High-Balance Disengagement Rate** → Premium churn risk  
- **Credit Card Stickiness Score** → Impact of credit card ownership  
- **Relationship Strength Index** → Combined engagement & product score

## 📈 Key Insights

- Customer engagement is the **strongest predictor of retention**
- Multi-product users show **lower churn rates**
- High-balance inactive customers are **high-risk segments**
- Behavioral analytics outperforms traditional financial metrics
